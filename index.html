Artifact 01 — Workshop 1 — Assignment 1.4

From Prompt to Product — Building Vera, an AI Startup Validator

AI Lab

A hands-on AI lab documenting the end-to-end process of designing, building, and iterating on a working AI chatbot — "Vera," a Startup Idea Validator — built on Chatbase.co using Design Thinking principles and structured prompt engineering. For an audience of technology leaders, this piece answers one question: can this person actually build something with AI, not just talk about it?

Objective

To gain direct hands-on experience with the full spectrum of AI tools — large language models, custom GPTs, research assistants, and chatbot builders — and to apply a Design Thinking framework to produce a functional, purpose-built AI product from scratch.

Description

Vera is an AI chatbot that stress-tests startup ideas through a structured five-step validation framework. A founder pitches in one sentence. Vera identifies the customer, probes why existing solutions fail, questions the revenue model, surfaces the single biggest risk, and delivers a structured verdict — all without sugarcoating.

Process

1

LLM Prompting: Designed a structured prompt (Context + Task + Constraints) and ran it across 3 separate ChatGPT-4o chats. Each surfaced a different critical insight — Microsoft Copilot threat, data cold-start problem. Lesson: always run multiple chats and look for where outputs diverge.

2

Custom GPT: Used Startup Mentor GPT to pressure-test go-to-market strategy. GPT recommended pivoting from B2C to B2B SaaS and focusing on one vertical — insights that directly shaped Vera's design philosophy.

3

STORM AI: Researched the AI productivity tools market. Discovered Reclaim.ai and Motion as closer competitors than anticipated. Identified privacy-first local processing as a potential differentiator.

4

Design Thinking: Empathy — founder with good idea but no honest feedback. Define — people get validation from people too nice to tell the truth. Ideate — stress-tester beats cheerleader. Prototype — Vera on Chatbase.co. Test — three startup ideas at different quality levels.

5

Build & Iterate: Iteration 1 too harsh. Iteration 2 added framing challenges as solvable problems. Iteration 3 added structured final verdict step — without it conversations ended inconclusively.
